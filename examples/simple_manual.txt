# Cooling Pump Maintenance Manual

## 1. System Overview

The CP-200 cooling pump circulates coolant through the primary heat exchanger. Its purpose is to maintain stable operating temperature during continuous machine operation.

The pump assembly includes an inlet valve, outlet valve, motor housing, pressure sensor, flow sensor, and removable filter cartridge.

The control system monitors pressure, flow rate, motor current, and coolant temperature. If any value moves outside the configured safety band, the controller raises an alarm.

## 2. Normal Operating Range

During normal operation, inlet pressure should remain between 180 and 220 kPa.

Outlet flow should remain between 38 and 45 litres per minute.

Coolant temperature should remain below 42 degrees Celsius during standard load and below 48 degrees Celsius during temporary peak load.

Motor current should remain below 7.5 amps. A sustained current above this value may indicate filter blockage, bearing wear, or increased mechanical resistance.

## 3. Startup Procedure

Before startup, confirm that the inlet valve is open and the outlet valve is partially open.

Check that the filter cartridge is seated correctly and that the filter cover is locked.

Start the pump from the control panel and observe pressure for thirty seconds.

If pressure rises rapidly but flow remains low, stop the pump and inspect the outlet valve and filter cartridge.

If pressure remains low and flow remains low, inspect the inlet valve, coolant reservoir level, and suction line.

## 4. Alarm Conditions

Alarm A12 indicates low flow. The most common causes are a blocked filter, closed outlet valve, failed flow sensor, or air trapped in the line.

Alarm A18 indicates high motor current. The most common causes are filter blockage, worn bearings, pump impeller damage, or incorrect coolant viscosity.

Alarm A21 indicates high coolant temperature. The most common causes are insufficient flow, heat exchanger fouling, or operation above rated load.

## 5. Maintenance Schedule

Inspect the filter cartridge every 250 operating hours.

Replace the filter cartridge every 1,000 operating hours or sooner if repeated low-flow alarms occur.

Inspect the inlet and outlet valves every 500 operating hours.

Record pressure, flow, coolant temperature, and motor current after each maintenance action.

## 6. Troubleshooting Notes

A low-flow alarm with high pressure usually indicates a downstream restriction.

A low-flow alarm with low pressure usually indicates an upstream restriction, low coolant level, or pump priming issue.

High motor current combined with falling flow may indicate mechanical resistance or severe filter blockage.

High coolant temperature with normal flow may indicate heat exchanger fouling rather than pump failure.
